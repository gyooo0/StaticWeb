ABOUT THE SERIES

GAME OF THRONES

Game of Thrones, based on the novels by George R. R. Martin, unfolds in the Seven Kingdoms of Westeros after King Robert's death sparks a ruthless battle for power among noble houses. At the heart of the story is House Stark of Winterfell, led by the honorable Eddard Stark, whose execution ignites civil war and scatters his family across the realm. Early in the series, the Stark children discover a litter of direwolves that become loyal companions and powerful symbols of their identity, strength, and connection to the North. Meanwhile, across the Narrow Sea, Daenerys Targaryen rises as the last heir of a fallen dynasty and becomes the Mother of dragons after hatching three legendary creatures—Drogon, Rhaegal, and Viserion—which symbolize the rebirth of Targaryen power and the return of magic to the world. As betrayal, war, and the looming threat of the White Walkers endanger the realm, the Starks endure loss, exile, and transformation, yet remain united by their shared heritage and the enduring spirit of the direwolf, representing loyalty, resilience, and the true heart of Winterfell.
