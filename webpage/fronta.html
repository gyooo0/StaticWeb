The Dire Wolves of House Stark

Grey Wind

Bonded to Robb Stark

Grey Wind was the dire wolf of Robb Stark, the King in the North. Named for his speed and smoke grey fur, Grey Wind was the largest and most aggressive of the direwolf litter. He fought alongside Robb in every battle during the War of the Five Kings, becoming a legend on the battlefield. His fierce and loyal temperament struck fear into the hearts of enemies. His battle prowess was unmatched among the dire wolves, but his fate was sealed at the Red Wedding, where he fell defending his master.

Lady

Bonded to Sansa Stark

Lady was the dire wolf of Sansa Stark, gentle and beautiful like her owner. With her silver grey fur and golden eyes, Lady embodied the grace and nobility expected of a highborn lady. She was the smallest of the litter and the most trusting, with a gentle temperament reflecting Sansa's own nature. Tragically, Lady became the first to fall, executed unjustly for a crime she did not commit, foreshadowing the hardships Sansa would endure.

Nymeria

Bonded to Arya Stark

Nymeria was named after the legendary warrior queen of the Rhoynar, reflecting Arya's own fierce and independent spirit. With dark grey fur and a wild temperament, she embodied freedom. When Arya was forced to drive her away to save her life, Nymeria fled into the wild and became the leader of a massive wolf pack in the Riverlands. She remained free and untamed, much like Arya herself, becoming a legend in her own right. The bond between them endured across the years, and Nymeria roams free to this day.

Summer

Bonded to Bran Stark

Summer was Bran Stark's dire wolf, named for the long summer that was ending when he was found. With silver grey fur and a protective, loyal temperament, Summer became Bran's protector after his fall. Through their profound warg bond, Bran could see through Summer's eyes and control his movements. Summer was fiercely protective of Bran, saving his life multiple times. Their bond deepened as Bran's powers grew, until Summer sacrificed himself defending Bran from the army of the dead.

Shaggydog

Bonded to Rickon Stark

Shaggydog was the dire wolf of Rickon Stark, the youngest of the Stark children. Named by the young Rickon, Shaggydog had jet black fur and was the most aggressive and unruly of all the dire wolves, with an untamed ferocity that reflected his master's wild nature after the trauma of losing his family. As Rickon grew more feral in his exile, so too did Shaggydog, becoming nearly impossible for anyone but Rickon to control. Their bond was primal and fierce, ending when Shaggydog was slain by the Umbers.

Ghost

Bonded to Jon Snow

Ghost was Jon Snow's albino dire wolf, the runt of the litter found separated from his siblings, much like Jon himself as a bastard. With pure white fur and red eyes, Ghost was silent and watchful, moving like a phantom through the snow. He was fiercely loyal to Jon, following him to the Wall and beyond. Ghost's uncanny ability to sense danger and his unbreakable bond with Jon made him invaluable. He was the last of the dire wolves to survive, embodying the endurance and resilience of House Stark.

The Dragons of Daenerys Targaryen

Drogon

Bonded to Daenerys Targaryen

Drogon was named after Daenerys's late husband, Khal Drogo, and was the largest and most aggressive of her three dragons. With black scales and red markings, his red-orange flames embodied the raw power and ferocity of House Targaryen. He was Daenerys's favorite and her personal mount, the one she rode into battle. Drogon's bond with the Mother of Dragons was the strongest, and he demonstrated intelligence and loyalty beyond that of a mere beast. He was the last dragon seen in Westeros, carrying Daenerys's body away after her death.

Rhaegal

Bonded to Daenerys Targaryen

Rhaegal was named in honor of Daenerys's brother, Rhaegar Targaryen, the fallen prince whose legacy haunted the realm. With green and bronze scales that shimmered like emeralds, and yellow-green flames, Rhaegal was a sight to behold. He was known for his agility and grace in flight. Though initially bonded to Daenerys, Rhaegal was later ridden by Jon Snow during the Great War against the Night King, symbolizing Jon's Targaryen heritage. His death at the hands of Euron Greyjoy's scorpion bolts was a devastating blow to Daenerys's forces.

Viserion

Bonded to Daenerys Targaryen

Viserion was named after Daenerys's brother Viserys, and bore cream and gold scales that gleamed in the sunlight. He breathed golden flames and was known for his beauty and slightly gentler temperament compared to his brothers. Viserion's fate took a dark turn when he was slain beyond the Wall by the Night King, only to be resurrected as an ice dragon with blue flames that could melt the ancient Wall itself. As the Night King's mount, he became a terrifying weapon against the living, until he was finally destroyed during the Battle of Winterfell.
